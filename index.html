Workout guide
⚡
Strides
What they areShort controlled accelerations done after your easy run, not during it.
How to run themBuild to ~85–90% max speed over 5 sec, hold for 10 sec, ease off over 5 sec. Smooth, not sprinting.
Recovery60–90 sec walk or very easy jog between each rep.
Treadmill tipStart pressing the speed up a few seconds early. 5.8–6.2 mph (9:40–10:20 /mi) for the stride portion.
Why they workWake up fast-twitch muscle fibers and reinforce good form with almost no fatigue cost.
↗
Hill repeats
Structure8 min warm-up → 4–6 reps uphill → 5 min cool-down.
How to run themStrong, controlled effort at ~80–85% max. Each rep lasts 45–60 sec. Eyes up, slight forward lean from ankles, drive your arms.
RecoveryDrop incline to 0% and walk 90 sec between reps. Don't rush it.
Treadmill settings4.8–5.0 mph (12:00–12:30 /mi) at 6% (wks 1–2) building to 8% (wks 5–6).
Why they workMimics hiking mechanics closely and builds leg strength without the pounding of flat speedwork.
⏱
Tempo runs
Structure10 min warm-up → 10–15 min tempo block → 5 min cool-down.
How to run them"Comfortably hard" — you can speak 3–4 words but not hold a conversation. Steady effort throughout, not a progression.
Treadmill settings5.3–5.6 mph (10:42–11:19 /mi) at 1% incline. Start at 5.1 mph if that feels too aggressive.
Warm-up and cool-downBoth at 4.5 mph (13:20 /mi), 1% incline. Genuinely easy — not just slightly slower than tempo.
Why they workTrains your lactate threshold — the pace you can sustain for longer efforts — with a short enough block to avoid digging into recovery.
